I get inspired by a lot of things, games and stories like Harvestella, Wuthering Waves, or Metaphor ReFantazio have given me some ideas as of late. But most of all, I think it’s the art that inspires me the most. The illustrations and art direction in these stories are so beautiful and fantastical. It’s amazing that anything can be drawn from nothing, and the feeling of endless creativity drives me to draw as well.




Themes like melancholy, nostalgia and childhood experiences are ideas I enjoy exploring the most. It could be things such as a walk with a friend after school, or the clear blue sky you see during the summer. In my mind, the normal and mundane can be so charming, and the memories we make in our youth become precious. I don't want all my work to be so somber however, since I like creating more fun and lighthearted animations/comics as well.




Despite digital art being my main medium, the artwork I want to create comes from traditional techniques such as visible brush strokes, varying texture, and even paint overs instead of the convenient eraser tool. I’m much more drawn to artwork that feels loose and imperfect, yet still clearly communicates its subject. What I strive to express from my art is a feeling. Whether that be forming a smile or a more bittersweet expression, I want my work to connect with others.
